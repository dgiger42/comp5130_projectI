Rubik's Cube Solver V1: (written in C++, finds inefficient solutions quickly)

Cube Solver V2: (written in Rust, finds optimal solutions, requires more time & uses more advanced data structures such as multi-layer Bloom filters designed to be safely modified in parallel using atomic operations. Finding optimal solutions is much more computationally intensive than finding inefficient solutions)

Cube Solver V3 (complete rewrite in Python, uses a neural network + beam search to find near-optimal solutions faster than the V2 version)

Prime Sieve Project: (not cube themed for a change). A highly optimized hybrid approach for finding prime numbers up to N. Combines two prime number generation techniques to achieve O(n) time while avoiding the poor constant factors typically associated with O(n) prime number generation techniques. Often outperforms primesieve (which is reputed to be the fastest prime number generation program) in single-threaded prime number generation, at least on the two machines I've tested it on. (I haven't made the repo public yet.)
